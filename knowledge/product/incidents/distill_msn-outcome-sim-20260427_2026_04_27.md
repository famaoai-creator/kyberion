# Outcome simulation surfaces 8 catalog gaps via 3-scenario hypothesis-tree

## Summary
Ran the hypothesis-tree pipeline against three contrasting scenarios (multi-tenant launch, MOS go/no-go, regulated counterfactual) to stress-test the Kyberion Intent Catalog and harvest 13 improvement points, 8 of which were promoted to §11 known gaps.

## Key Learnings
- Three-scenario triangulation (growth / decision-gate / regulated) exposes catalog blind spots that single-scenario reviews miss — divergent contexts force orthogonal failure modes to surface.
- 27 hypotheses + 18 critiques is a productive yield ratio for a 3-scenario fanout; the cross-critique stage is what converts raw hypotheses into actionable improvement points.
- Routing simulation outputs directly into the intent-catalog §11 known-gaps section closes the loop between divergent reasoning and the governed artifact, preventing learnings from stranding in mission evidence.

## Patterns Discovered
- Outcome-simulation-as-audit: treat the hypothesis-tree pipeline as a structured red-team against any catalog/spec, with scenario diversity (not scenario count) as the quality lever.
- Improvement-point promotion gate: extract IP-1..IP-N to evidence/, then promote a subset to the canonical artifact's known-gaps section — keeps the catalog stable while preserving full reasoning trail.

## Reusable Artifacts
- evidence/improvement-points.md (IP-1..IP-13 extraction template)
- pipelines/hypothesis-tree applied to 3-scenario fanout configuration
- intent-catalog §11 known-gaps promotion pattern (8 of 13 IPs surfaced)
